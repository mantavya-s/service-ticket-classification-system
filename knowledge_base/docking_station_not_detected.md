# Docking Station Not Detected

## Category
Hardware

## Subcategory
Docking Station / USB-C

## Symptoms
Users may report that a docking station does not provide power, network, USB, audio, or display connectivity.

Common signs include:
- The laptop does not charge through the dock
- Monitors, keyboard, mouse, or ethernet are not detected
- Only some dock functions work
- The dock works intermittently
- The issue starts after reconnecting the laptop or returning from sleep
- The dock works with another laptop but not the affected laptop

## Likely Causes
- The USB-C or Thunderbolt cable is loose or damaged
- The dock power adapter is disconnected
- The laptop is connected to a USB-C port that does not support docking
- Dock firmware or drivers are outdated
- USB-C or Thunderbolt permissions are not approved
- The dock is frozen and requires a power cycle
- The laptop USB-C port or dock hardware has failed

## Troubleshooting Steps
1. Confirm the dock power adapter is connected and the dock power light is on.
2. Disconnect the laptop from the dock.
3. Disconnect power from the dock for at least 30 seconds.
4. Reconnect dock power, then reconnect the laptop.
5. Confirm the correct USB-C or Thunderbolt port is being used.
6. Inspect the cable and ports for physical damage.
7. Test another compatible USB-C or Thunderbolt cable if available.
8. Restart the laptop.
9. Check Device Manager for USB, Thunderbolt, or dock-related errors.
10. Install the manufacturer dock driver and firmware updates.
11. Test the dock with another compatible laptop.
12. Test the laptop with another known working dock.

## Escalation Notes
Escalate if:
- The dock receives no power
- The dock fails with multiple compatible laptops
- The laptop fails with multiple known working docks
- The USB-C port is physically damaged
- Firmware updates fail
- The issue affects multiple docks of the same model

Possible escalation team:
Desktop Support / Hardware Support

## Suggested Ticket Comment
I power-cycled the docking station, verified the USB-C or Thunderbolt connection, and tested the dock and laptop with alternate equipment. I also checked whether the required dock drivers and firmware were installed.

The issue will be escalated if testing confirms a failed dock, cable, or laptop port.
