# Laptop Screen Is Black

## Category
Hardware

## Subcategory
Laptop Display / Power

## Symptoms
Users may report that the laptop appears to be powered on, but the built-in screen remains black.

Common signs include:
- Power lights or keyboard lights are on
- The fan can be heard
- The screen is completely black or extremely dim
- The laptop displays correctly on an external monitor
- The issue begins after sleep, docking, or a Windows update
- The laptop restarts but the display does not return

## Likely Causes
- Screen brightness is turned down
- The laptop is sending video to an external display
- The graphics driver is frozen
- The laptop is stuck in sleep or hibernation
- The internal display cable is loose or damaged
- The screen backlight has failed
- The battery or power system is not supplying stable power
- The motherboard or graphics hardware has failed

## Troubleshooting Steps
1. Confirm the laptop is connected to power.
2. Increase the screen brightness using the keyboard controls.
3. Press `Windows + Ctrl + Shift + B` to reset the graphics driver.
4. Press `Windows + P` and cycle through the display modes.
5. Disconnect all docks, monitors, USB devices, and accessories.
6. Hold the power button for approximately 10 to 15 seconds to force a shutdown.
7. Restart the laptop with only the power adapter connected.
8. Connect the laptop to a known working external monitor.
9. Shine a flashlight at the laptop screen to check for a faint image, which may indicate a backlight failure.
10. If the battery is removable, perform a power drain according to the device support procedure.
11. Record whether the manufacturer logo or BIOS screen appears during startup.

## Escalation Notes
Escalate if:
- No manufacturer logo appears during startup
- The laptop works only with an external monitor
- A faint image is visible but the backlight does not work
- The laptop has been dropped or exposed to liquid
- The issue returns after a graphics driver reset
- Hardware diagnostics report a failure

Possible escalation team:
Desktop Support / Hardware Repair

## Suggested Ticket Comment
I disconnected external devices, reset the graphics output, checked the brightness and display mode, and tested the laptop with an external monitor. The results indicate whether the issue is related to Windows, the graphics driver, the internal screen, or the laptop hardware.
The ticket will be escalated for hardware diagnostics or repair if the built-in display continues to remain black.
