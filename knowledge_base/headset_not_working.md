# Headset Not Working

## Category
Hardware

## Subcategory
Headset / Audio Device

## Symptoms
Users may report that a USB, Bluetooth, or 3.5 mm headset is not producing sound, the microphone is not working, or the headset is not detected.

Common signs include:
- The user cannot hear audio through the headset
- Other participants cannot hear the user
- The headset does not appear in sound settings
- Audio continues through the laptop speakers
- The headset works in one application but not another
- The headset disconnects or cuts out during calls

## Likely Causes
- The wrong input or output device is selected
- The headset is muted
- The USB port, audio jack, or Bluetooth connection is not working
- The application is using a different audio device than Windows
- The headset cable or connector is damaged
- Audio drivers are outdated or frozen
- Bluetooth battery level is low
- Another application has exclusive control of the microphone

## Troubleshooting Steps
1. Confirm the headset is connected and powered on.
2. Check for a physical mute switch or inline mute button.
3. Disconnect and reconnect the headset.
4. Try a different USB port or audio jack.
5. Open Windows Sound Settings and select the headset as both the input and output device.
6. Use the microphone test in Sound Settings.
7. Check the audio settings inside Teams, Zoom, Skype, or the affected application.
8. Close other applications that may be using the microphone.
9. Disable and re-enable the headset in Device Manager.
10. For Bluetooth headsets, remove the device and pair it again.
11. Test the headset on another computer.
12. Test a known working headset on the affected computer.
13. Update the audio, Bluetooth, or USB drivers if required.

## Escalation Notes
Escalate if:
- The headset fails on multiple computers
- Multiple headsets fail on the same computer
- The audio device repeatedly disappears from Device Manager
- The computer has a damaged USB port or audio jack
- Driver reinstallation does not resolve the issue

Possible escalation team:
Desktop Support / Hardware Support

## Suggested Ticket Comment
I verified the headset connection, checked the mute controls, and confirmed the correct microphone and speaker were selected in both Windows and the affected application. I also tested the headset and computer with alternate equipment to determine whether the problem follows the headset or the workstation.
The issue will be escalated if a hardware replacement or driver-level repair is required.
