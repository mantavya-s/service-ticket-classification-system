# Lost or Stolen Organizational Device

## Category
Security

## Subcategory
Device Security Incident

## Symptoms
A user reports that an organizational laptop, phone, tablet, security key, or other managed device is missing or stolen.

Common signs include:
- The user cannot locate the device
- The device was left in a public location
- A bag containing organizational equipment was stolen
- The device contains locally stored organizational information
- The missing device may still be signed in
- A Duo device or security token is also missing

## Likely Causes
- The device was misplaced
- The device was stolen
- The user left the device in transit, a meeting room, or a public location
- The device was transferred without proper asset tracking
- The device was returned to another department without notice

## Troubleshooting Steps
1. Record the device type, asset tag, serial number, assigned user, and last known location.
2. Record the approximate time the device was last seen.
3. Ask whether the device was powered on, encrypted, or signed in.
4. Tell the user not to attempt recovery from an unsafe location.
5. Immediately notify the security or incident response team.
6. Disable or revoke active sessions according to the incident procedure.
7. Disable a missing security key or Duo device if applicable.
8. Initiate remote lock or wipe only through authorized device management.
9. Determine whether sensitive information was stored locally.
10. Record any police or security report number if one exists.
11. Update the asset management record.

## Escalation Notes
Escalate immediately in all confirmed lost or stolen device cases.

Possible escalation team:
Security Operations / Incident Response / Endpoint Management / Asset Management

## Suggested Ticket Comment

The lost or stolen device has been documented with the available asset and location details. The appropriate security and endpoint-management teams have been notified, and account sessions or authentication devices will be secured according to the incident response procedure.
Further updates will be added as the device status and data exposure risk are assessed.
