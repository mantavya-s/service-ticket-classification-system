# Monitor / Display / TV Not Working

## Category
Hardware

## Subcategory
Monitor / External Display

## Symptoms
Users may report that a monitor, television, or meeting room display is blank, shows "No Signal," or is not detected by the computer.

Common signs include:
- The computer is powered on, but nothing appears on the external display
- The display shows "No Signal" or "Input Not Found"
- The laptop screen works, but the connected monitor or TV does not
- The image appears on the wrong display
- The display works intermittently
- The issue started after changing a cable, dock, adapter, or workstation

## Likely Causes
- The monitor or TV is powered off
- The wrong HDMI, DisplayPort, or input source is selected
- The video cable is loose, damaged, or connected to the wrong port
- A USB-C dock or adapter is not passing video correctly
- Windows display settings are set to "PC screen only"
- The display driver is frozen or outdated
- The monitor, dock, cable, or computer port has failed

## Troubleshooting Steps
1. Confirm the monitor or TV is powered on.
2. Confirm the correct input source is selected on the display.
3. Disconnect and firmly reconnect the video cable at both ends.
4. Press `Windows + P` and select Duplicate or Extend.
5. Open Display Settings and select Detect under Multiple Displays.
6. Restart the computer and power-cycle the monitor or TV.
7. Test a different HDMI or DisplayPort cable.
8. Test a different video port, dock port, or adapter.
9. Connect the computer directly to the display to bypass the dock.
10. Test the display with another computer, or test the computer with another display.
11. Update or reinstall the graphics and docking-station drivers if applicable.
12. Record which component fails during testing.

## Escalation Notes
Escalate if:
- The display does not work with multiple known working cables
- The computer cannot detect any external display
- The issue follows the monitor, dock, or computer during testing
- Physical damage is visible on a port or cable
- The graphics driver repeatedly crashes
- Multiple displays in the same room stop working

Possible escalation team:
Desktop Support / Hardware Support / Audio-Visual Support
