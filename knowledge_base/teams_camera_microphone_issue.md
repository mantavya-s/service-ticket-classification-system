# Teams Camera or Microphone Not Working

## Category
Software

## Subcategory
Microsoft Teams

## Symptoms
Users may report that Microsoft Teams cannot access the camera or microphone even though the device is connected.

Common signs include:
- The camera preview is black
- Teams reports that no camera or microphone is available
- The microphone works in Windows but not in Teams
- Another application can use the camera but Teams cannot
- The user cannot unmute
- The device stops working after docking or reconnecting peripherals

## Likely Causes
- The wrong camera or microphone is selected in Teams
- Windows privacy permissions are blocking Teams
- Another application is using the device
- The device driver is outdated or frozen
- Teams cache or application files are corrupted
- A browser is blocking camera or microphone access
- A physical privacy shutter or microphone mute switch is enabled

## Troubleshooting Steps
1. Confirm the physical camera shutter is open and the microphone is not muted.
2. Open Teams Settings and select the correct camera, microphone, and speaker.
3. Use the Teams test call feature.
4. Check Windows camera and microphone privacy permissions.
5. Close Zoom, Skype, browser tabs, or other applications using the devices.
6. Disconnect and reconnect the camera or headset.
7. Restart Teams.
8. Restart the computer.
9. Test the camera and microphone in Windows Camera and Sound Settings.
10. Update Teams and the device drivers.
11. Clear the Teams cache according to the approved support procedure.
12. Test Teams in a browser or on another computer.

## Escalation Notes
Escalate if:
- The device is not detected by Windows
- Multiple cameras or microphones fail on the same computer
- The issue is caused by an organizational policy
- Teams continues to fail after cache clearing and reinstallation
- The issue affects multiple users

Possible escalation team:
Desktop Support / Microsoft 365 Administration

## Suggested Ticket Comment
I verified the camera and microphone settings in Teams, checked Windows privacy permissions, closed other applications using the devices, and tested the hardware outside Teams. I also refreshed the Teams application and device drivers where required.
The ticket will be escalated if the issue is related to device management or Microsoft 365 policy.
